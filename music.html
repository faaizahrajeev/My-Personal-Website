While My Guitar Gently Weeps- Live

I've been listening to this performance on repeat for the last three months. You know it's a banger when you pause your Spotify or Apple playlist and head to youtube for that one song. This Beatles song was written by George Harrison and it conveys his dismay at the world's potential for universal love, which he refers to as "the love there that's sleeping". This performance by Eric Clapton, Ringo Starr, and Paul McCartney was held at the Royal Albert Hall in London on 29 November 2002 as a memorial to George Harrison on the first anniversary of his death.

I Hear a Symphony

After hearing the orchestral cover of Eleanor Rigby by Cody Fry, I stumbled upon his song- I Hear a Symphony. It gave me the same orchestral feel as Eleanor Rigby and after a thousand listens, I still get chills when the song reaches the instrumental ensemble. It has 3 verses and no chorus, just an orchestral melody that serves as the interlude between each verse. It expresses how life becomes colorful once you find your loved one.

Don't Look Back In Anger
